Open-source utility

RevBridge

A maintained, friendly way to share a computer’s internet connection with Android over USB—without root.

How it works

The Android companion creates a local VPN interface. Packets cross the USB debugging link, and the native relay opens normal connections on this computer.

Privacy by design

There is no RevBridge cloud service, account, analytics, or remote server. Traffic leaves through the computer’s existing internet connection.

Credits and licence

RevBridge is derived from Genymobile’s gnirehtet and keeps its Apache License 2.0 attribution. It is independent and not endorsed by Genymobile.

Supported systems

Windows, Linux, and macOS desktop builds; Android 5.0 and newer on the connected device. IPv4 TCP and UDP are supported.
